Industry Data


The industry data supplied by Burning Glass was a very interesting insight to the IT industry regarding job roles and the skills requested for successful candidates to possess. The ideal job titles of the group IT manager, software engineering program manager, software developer flight control, mid-level penetration test, cyber security analyst, front end developer. According to the burning glass results, Andrew’s ideal job as an IT manager is a very similar job to a systems administrator and it ranks in the top ten roles with a total of 681 advertised roles between
March 24th 2017 and March 23rd 2018[2]. What was surprising was cyber security roles like the ideal roles of Benjamin as penetration tester and Kieren as cyber security analyst was that neither of them were found in the top ranked listing nor were there any roles similar to either of these ideal job roles.

Through analysis of the top ranked generic skills from the burning glass data[1], it was seen the most common required skills in the group were: problem solving, team work/collaboration, communication skills, planning, writing, and time management. All these generic skills are found in the top 6 of greatest in demand by employers. From the knowledge gathered from this it can be assumed that the team’s ideal roles require a high level of business and people understanding with senior management traits in order to be chosen in recruitment and successful while in that role.

However, when it came to IT-specific skills, the top 6 most common in the group were found throughout the results. The top 6 most common IT-specific skills in the group are: software engineering, git, javascript, python, building relationships, and project management[3]. These skills are all found in the top half of the burning glass results and suggest they are highly sought after and required by potential employers. Regarding the IT-specific skills results provided by burning glass the three highest ranked skills that are not in team required skillset are: business process, SAP and business management.
